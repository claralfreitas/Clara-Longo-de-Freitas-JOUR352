The Howard Center of Investigative Journalism is a multidisciplinary program at Phillip Merrill College of Journalism that allows students to work with news organizations in investigative projects.

I started to work with the center Jan. of 2020 as a reporter for a national project that partnered with six other universities - University of Oregon, Boston University, Stanford University, the University of Arkansas, the University of Florida and Arizona State University - and AP News.

It was arduous, yet refreshing, experience. It was my first time working with data, court records and local, municipal and state laws. I had to interview city officials, law enforcement agents and people experiencing homelessness.

And then, the COVID-19 pandemic hit.

As the phase of the project wrapped up, the center had to migrate all its efforts to an online environment. I continued to report, write, edit and fact-check from home. The entire world was in a pretty difficult - and, as you are probably tired of reading, unprecendented - situation. But it all paid off.
